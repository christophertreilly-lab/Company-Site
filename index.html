What I Do

Services

A focused range of forensic social work services for courts, attorneys, and the people they represent.

PC §1001.36 Mental Health Diversion Assessments

I evaluate whether someone qualifies for Mental Health Diversion under California Penal Code §1001.36. My assessments are thorough, clearly written, and accepted across multiple California courts.

Court Accepted

Social History Reports

I write reports that give the court a thorough understanding of a person's background — trauma history, family context, mental health, and substance use — written clearly for a judicial audience.

Defense Focused

Drug Court Case Management

I manage Drug Court cases from intake through graduation, tracking phase progression, coordinating with treatment providers, and keeping attorneys and the court informed along the way.

Multi County

ASAM Assessments and Treatment Planning

Substance use and mental health assessments grounded in the ASAM framework, with individualized treatment plans built around each person's clinical needs.

CADC III Credentialed

Progress Reports and Court Documentation

Ongoing documentation of a client's progress in treatment or diversion, written clearly for judges and attorneys with appropriate clinical context.

Ongoing Support

Attorney Consultation

I am available for consultation with defense attorneys to help identify appropriate diversion options and determine the best approach for a given case.

Who I Work With

Who I work with

Forensic social work touches a lot of people. Here is how I can help each of them.

Defense Attorneys

I work with defense attorneys on diversion eligibility, social history reports, and Drug Court case management. I aim to be responsive and to provide documentation that is useful in court.

Courts and Judges

My reports are written to be clear and useful to judicial audiences. I try to present clinical information in plain language and ground my work in California law and established clinical frameworks.

Defendants and Families

If you or someone you love is involved in a criminal case alongside mental health or addiction issues, I can help explain what options may be available and what the process generally looks like.

Forensic Colleagues

I work alongside treatment providers, program staff, and other forensic professionals. I try to communicate consistently across all parties involved in a client's case.

About

The person behind the work

I'm a Licensed Clinical Social Worker and Certified Addiction Drug Counselor with over ten years of clinical social work experience, including several years focused on forensic practice across California's court system.

My forensic work has centered on PC §1001.36 Mental Health Diversion assessments, Drug Court case management, and social history and mitigation reports. I have completed court accepted assessments across five counties and work regularly with attorneys, treatment providers, and court staff.

I also work as a clinical social worker at Kaiser Permanente. That role keeps my clinical skills current and informs how I approach forensic work.

I believe that context matters in every case, and I try to provide courts and attorneys with a thorough, honest picture of the people I work with.
